The Problem

Our RMs are walking into meetings under-prepared

Every client meeting requires intelligence from a dozen disconnected sources. Today, that research takes 30–60 minutes — if it happens at all. The result: missed signals, missed opportunities, and missed revenue.

  • 30–60 minutes of manual pre-meeting research per client, per meeting
  • Fragmented sources — SEC filings, news, LinkedIn, company sites in separate siloes
  • Stale intelligence — no automatic freshness tracking; outdated data reaches the meeting room
  • Shallow synthesis — raw data arrives without AI-generated recommendations or officer profiles
  • No pipeline visibility — RMs have no consolidated view of their portfolio's health, deal triggers, or contact recency
  • Incumbent blind spot — who is the client's current lead bank? Often unknown before the pitch
  • Relationship signals missed — board interlocks and alumni ties that could warm a cold call go undetected
The Solution

Research Engine

9 AI Agents. One Unified Graph.

WebScraperAgent

Dimension 1
EdgarAgent
Downloads 10-K / 10-Q from SEC EDGAR — extracts revenue, net income, assets, cash, equity with ticker normalisation
Dimension 2
NewsAgent + Classifier
Fetches recent headlines via DuckDuckGo, classifies each by sentiment, severity, materiality, and event type using Claude
Dimension 3
ProductAgent
Maps likely banking product needs (Treasury, Credit, Capital Markets, etc.) to client sector and size profile
Dimension 4
IndustryAgent
Assigns NAICS classification, identifies peer companies with tickers, surfaces sector trends and key drivers
Dimension 5 + 6
OfficerAgent
Discovers and deep-profiles executives via SEC proxy, web, and Wikipedia — risk flags, LinkedIn, board seats, confidence scoring. Always runs Claude Sonnet 4.6.
Dimension 7
TemporalDimension
Applies freshness decay curves to all dimensions — prunes stale data, boosts recent material events, scores relevance per item
Cross-cutting
Neo4j / LLM
Merges all dimensions into a connected knowledge graph; generates AI executive summary across all 7 dimensions

Section Three
RM Workflow Features
Eight purpose-built features that move the RM from passive reader to active deal-maker — before, during, and after every client interaction.
Feature 1 of 8

Deal Trigger Alerts

Claude reads every news item and financial data point for the client and classifies them into deal trigger types — so the RM knows what product to lead with before they pick up the phone.

  • M&A Activity — acquisition language in filings or press releases surfaces a capital raise or bridge loan opportunity
  • Capital Raise — mentions of expansion, capex, or new markets flags equity/debt appetite
  • Debt Refinancing — upcoming maturity or credit downgrade triggers a refinancing conversation
  • Leadership Transition — new CFO or CEO means fresh relationships to establish
  • Distress Signals — covenant breach risk or negative news elevates risk monitoring priority
  • Regulatory Events — compliance change flags risk management products

High Urgency

Triggers rated HIGH animate in the dashboard header to ensure the RM acts before the next scheduled call.

Each trigger includes

  • Trigger type and title
  • Evidence from news or financials
  • Urgency level (high / medium / low)
  • Recommended WF product
  • Suggested next action
Feature 2 of 8

Feature 4 of 8

Incumbent Bank Detection

Before pitching, the RM needs to know who they are replacing — or who they are competing with. Context Fabric searches SEC credit agreement language and public sources to identify the client's current banking relationships.

  • Administrative Agent / Lead Bank — the primary relationship bank identified from credit agreement filings
  • Other Lenders / Arrangers — syndicate members and co-arrangers with current exposure
  • Facility Details — facility type (revolver, term loan), size, and maturity date where available
  • WF Already Involved — highlighted in green if Wells Fargo appears in the syndicate
  • Opportunity Note — Claude summarises the specific opportunity for WF given the current structure
  • Source Links — direct links to the SEC filings or web sources used

Confidence Score

Every detection is rated High / Medium / Low confidence based on source quality. RMs know exactly how much to rely on each result.

Surfaces in

  • Pitch tab — loads on demand, not on every page load
  • Refresh button to re-run against updated filings
  • Used alongside Recommendations and Warm Entry Points
Feature 5 of 8

Officer Intelligence + Relationship Map

Officer Intelligence

  • Auto-profiled during every research run — no extra steps
  • Background summary, education, previous roles, tenure
  • Risk flags (litigation, regulatory action, controversy)
  • Banking relevance note — why this officer matters to WF
  • Key achievements, board seats, publications
  • Confidence score per profile (high / medium / low)
  • Manual re-profile any officer with one click

Board Interlock Map

Shows every external board seat shared between a client officer and a Wells Fargo officer — grouped by board. A shared board seat is a warm introduction that most bankers never know exists.

Alumni Network

Identifies client officers who attended the same institution as a WF board member or executive. Fuzzy matching handles abbreviations. Grouped by school. Surfaces the WF contact name and role for the introduction ask.

17 Wells Fargo officers are seeded in the graph with real board memberships and education data. Every client research run automatically cross-references against this network.

Feature 6 of 8

RM Activity Log + Prior Deals History

Activity Log

  • Log calls, emails, meetings, and notes against the client
  • Each entry: contact name, role, date, notes, and next action
  • Last contact date surfaced prominently — red if over 60 days
  • All activity persists in Neo4j across sessions

Prior Deals History

  • Record WF products by category: Treasury, Credit, Capital Markets, Trade Finance, Risk Management, Deposits
  • Status tracking: pipeline / active / closed / lost
  • Amount, start/close date, and deal notes
  • Active deal count surfaces on Portfolio Dashboard
Summary Bar
At a glance per client
Total activities  ·  Active deals  ·  Pipeline count  ·  Last contact days ago

Why this matters

Today the RM's activity and deal history lives in email, Outlook notes, and memory. Context Fabric ties it directly to the intelligence profile — so the next RM who covers this client starts informed, not from scratch.
